🎫 Which Ticket for Which Transport
Oyster Card / Contactless — tap in & tap out
All Underground (Tube) lines · Elizabeth line (Crossrail) · London Overground · DLR · Most TfL buses. London buses are fully cashless — Oyster or contactless only, no cash accepted. Daily spend cap auto-applies (~£8.10 Zones 1–2). Kids under 11 ride free with a paying adult on any TfL service.
Heathrow Express — separate ticket required
NOT on Oyster. Buy at heathrowexpress.com in advance (from £22) vs. £37+ walk-up at the station. Alternative: Piccadilly line from any Heathrow terminal accepts Oyster — ~50 min, ~£6, much cheaper, and goes directly to central London.
National Rail (trains leaving London) — separate tickets
• Paddington → Windsor & Eton Central (GWR, via Slough): buy at station or gwr.com · ~£12 return per person
• St Pancras → Canterbury West (Southeastern High Speed): book at southeasternrailway.co.uk — advance tickets significantly cheaper; book as soon as dates open
Note: Some National Rail journeys within London Zones accept Oyster — check "Oyster on National Rail" at booking.
Taxis & Uber
Black cabs: cash or card (no Oyster). Uber: app payment. No car seats legally required for under-12s in licensed taxis or minicabs on shorter Kent journeys — but bring a booster if you prefer.
🎡 Go City London Explorer Pass — Is It Worth It?
What it is: A pre-paid bundle where you pick N attractions from a pool of 107+ options. Valid for 30 days from first use. Entry via mobile app — no paper tickets needed at the gate. Buy at: gocity.com/en/london/passes/explorer. Free cancellation within 90 days of purchase if plans change.
Pass sizes & adult prices (2026):
3 attractions: £89/adult · 5 attractions: £129/adult · 7 attractions: £159/adult
Child pricing is lower — check site for current rates. Kids under 3 typically free.

The math for this trip:
LEGOLAND + Tower of London + Tower Bridge individually = ~£110–120/adult vs. £89 for a 3-choice pass — already a clear saving. Adding City Cruises + Golden Hinde brings you to 5 choices (£129 pass) vs. ~£148 standalone. If you also want London Eye or Westminster Abbey, a 7-choice pass at £159 is easily worth it. Bottom line: the pass pays for itself on LEGOLAND + Tower of London alone.
Important: Natural History Museum is free (no pass needed). Leeds Castle is NOT on the Explorer Pass — separate ticket. Diana Playground is free. Even with the Explorer Pass, LEGOLAND requires a timed-entry booking at legoland.co.uk — do this right after purchasing the pass.

SCRIPTURE

“My heart is steadfast, O God! I will sing and make melody with all my being! Awake, O harp and lyre! I will awake the dawn! I will give thanks to you, O Lord, among the peoples; I will sing praises to you among the nations. For your steadfast love is great above the heavens; your faithfulness reaches to the clouds. Be exalted, O God, above the heavens! Let your glory be over all the earth! That your beloved ones may be delivered, give salvation by your right hand and answer me! God has promised in his holiness: "With exultation I will divide up Shechem and portion out the Valley of Succoth. Gilead is mine; Manasseh is mine; Ephraim is my helmet, Judah my scepter. Moab is my washbasin; upon Edom I cast my shoe; over Philistia I shout in triumph." Who will bring me to the fortified city? Who will lead me to Edom? Have you not rejected us, O God? You do not go out, O God, with our armies. Oh grant us help against the foe, for vain is the salvation of man! With God we shall do valiantly; it is he who will tread down our foes.”

OBSERVATION

  • My heart is steadfast
  • Praise you
  • Sing of you
  • Great is your love
  • Faithfulness reaches to the clouds
  • Exalted —> Glory (weight) be over the earth
  • Save us and help us —> deliver us
  • Vain is the salvation of man vs salvation of God
  • With God, we shall [go] valiantly

APPLICATION

The salvation of man is:

  • Temporary, not enduring
  • Rises up only to the level of value that a person has intrinsically
  • Inconsistent

The salvation of God is:

  • Enduring, lasts for eternity
  • Faithful
  • Loving
  • Confers the glory (weight) of God
  • Allows my heart to be steadfast

What does the salvation of man look like? As an example, it looks like using your promotion at work to feel better about yourself. The promotion itself is not bad; it is conferred when you are doing a good job, are performing competently at the next level, and are delivering value to your organization.


It’s the part where you look at that promotion and tell your heart that you are a better person because of it — that’s the salvation of man. The only person who can confer the value of being a better person is the one who is actually better. And the only person who is actually better is God. And God only confers that value in one way — that way is Jesus, whose value not only makes us better, but makes us perfect. And that is the salvation of God — to become perfect through his son.


But there’s a problem with comprehending the goodness of God. I can comprehend the value of a promotion in my compensation, the respect earned from my peers, and in the expectations placed upon me. Even if God’s goodness is ultimate, my ability to comprehend His goodness can only rise up to the level of my experience or the level of goodness to which I can empathize. That level is not very high.


PRAYER

Lord, we pray then for two things:

  1. To experience greater and greater experiences of your goodness.
  2. To be revealed to the goodness that is around us, but that we are blind to.

The extent to which our comprehension of your goodness surpasses our pull toward the beauty and goodness of this world will dictate how hungry we are for the things not in this world — in increasingly desperate reliance on you.
